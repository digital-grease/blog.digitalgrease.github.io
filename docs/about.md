# About The Forge

I'm digitalgrease. Cybersecurity engineer, hacker, maker. I grew up rural and poor with an internet connection and too much curiosity, became a father in my teens, and spent over a decade in cities chasing the version of opportunity that density promises. I've built a career in security and software engineering — vulnerability management, infrastructure, architecture — the kind of work where patience and ingenuity matter more than speed.

A couple years ago, my partner and I moved back to rural land. The forge started glowing not long after.

This blog is where both halves meet.

---

## What You'll Find Here

**Digital** — Security research, offensive tooling, engineering write-ups. The kind of work that lives in terminals and threat models. I build tools like [EVE](https://github.com/digital-grease/EVE) (VoIP covert channels) and [Fauxx](https://github.com/digital-grease/fauxx) (ad-tech data poisoning) and write about them here — not just what they do, but how they think.

**Analog** — Blacksmithing, farming, building things with your hands. Walipini greenhouses, cedar enclosures, sheep and chickens. The work that happens after the screen goes dark and the forge starts to glow.

**Prose** — Essays on technology, culture, nostalgia, and the strange experience of watching the world rewire itself in real time. The kind of writing that happens when you sit with an idea long enough to find out what it actually means.

Hot metal and code are more alike than people think. Both do exactly what you tell them, not what you mean. Both will expose a gap in your understanding immediately and without sympathy. The forge doesn't care about your intentions any more than the compiler does.

## The Shape of This Place

I'm drawn to the quiet places where systems reveal their character. A forge burning in the dark like the phosphor glow of an old CRT, both casting the same patient light on whatever truth you're trying to shape. This blog is a living notebook — experiments, half-finished ideas, problems solved the hard way, and the occasional breakthrough.

If you want the full picture: [digitalgrease.dev](https://digitalgrease.dev)

*Memento mori. Act with purpose. Leave a clean signal in the noise.*
